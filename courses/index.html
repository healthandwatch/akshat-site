Mathematics Courses

Course Archives & Materials

Course Overview

The following are archives for various upper-division mathematics courses. These archives serve as catalogs of definitions, theorems, and concepts discussed in each course, and do not substitute for a proper textbook. Each course page contains an overview of key topics, learning objectives, and recommended resources.

Available Courses

  • ■ Math 120: Introduction to Abstract Algebra
    Explore the fundamental structures of abstract algebra, including groups, rings, and fields. Learn about homomorphisms, isomorphisms, and their applications.
  • ■ Math 121: Linear Algebra
    Study vector spaces, matrices, eigenvalues, and their applications across mathematics and science. Essential for computational and theoretical work.
  • ■ Math 130: Probability
    Understand probability theory, random variables, distributions, and statistical inference. Foundations for modern data science and random phenomena.
  • ■ Math 140: Elementary Analysis
    Rigorous treatment of real analysis, including limits, continuity, derivatives, and integrals. The mathematical foundation of calculus.

Additional Reading

The following books and resources are collections of essays exploring various aspects of mathematical reasoning that may prove interesting:

  • When Einstein Walked With Gödel: Excursions to the Edge of Thought by Jim Holt
  • The Outer Limits of Reason: What Science, Mathematics, and Logic Cannot Tell Us by Noson S. Yanofsky
  • Ruelle's Essays
