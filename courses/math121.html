Math 121: Linear Algebra

This course studies vector spaces, linear transformations, and their applications across mathematics and science.

Course Overview

Linear algebra is fundamental to modern mathematics and has applications in nearly every scientific field. In this course, students will explore:

  • Vector spaces and subspaces
  • Linear dependence and independence
  • Basis and dimension
  • Linear transformations and matrices
  • Eigenvalues and eigenvectors
  • Diagonalization and normal forms

Key Concepts

This course emphasizes both theoretical understanding and practical computation:

  • Vector Spaces: Abstract spaces satisfying addition and scalar multiplication axioms
  • Matrix Operations: Multiplication, inversion, and decomposition
  • Eigenanalysis: Understanding the behavior of linear systems

Applications

Linear algebra is essential for applications in:

  • Computer graphics and image processing
  • Quantum mechanics and physics
  • Machine learning and data science
  • Engineering and control theory

Resources

Comprehensive notes, worked examples, and problem sets for this course are available upon request. For additional reading, consult "Linear Algebra Done Right" by Sheldon Axler or "Introduction to Linear Algebra" by Gilbert Strang.
