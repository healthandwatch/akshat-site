Math 120: Introduction to Abstract Algebra

This course provides an introduction to the fundamental structures of abstract algebra.

Course Overview

In this course, students will explore:

  • Group theory and group structures
  • Ring theory and polynomial rings
  • Field theory and field extensions
  • Homomorphisms and isomorphisms
  • Subgroups, subrings, and ideals

Key Concepts

Abstract algebra is the study of algebraic structures with operations. The course focuses on understanding:

  • Groups: Sets with an associative binary operation
  • Rings: Groups under addition with multiplication
  • Fields: Rings where every nonzero element has a multiplicative inverse

Resources

Comprehensive notes and problem sets for this course are available upon request. For additional reading on abstract algebra, consult standard textbooks such as "Abstract Algebra" by David S. Dummit and Richard M. Foote.
