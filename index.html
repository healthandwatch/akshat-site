Mathematics Course Archives

A comprehensive collection of course materials and resources for upper-division mathematics courses.

Course List

The following are archives for various upper-division mathematics courses. These archives serve as catalogs of definitions, theorems, and concepts discussed in each course, and do not substitute for a proper textbook.

  • ■ Math 120: Introduction to Abstract Algebra
    Explore the fundamental structures of abstract algebra, including groups, rings, and fields.
  • ■ Math 121: Linear Algebra
    Study vector spaces, matrices, eigenvalues, and their applications across mathematics and science.
  • ■ Math 130: Probability
    Understand probability theory, random variables, distributions, and statistical inference.
  • ■ Math 140: Elementary Analysis
    Rigorous treatment of real analysis, including limits, continuity, derivatives, and integrals.

Additional Reading

The following books and resources are collections of essays exploring various aspects of mathematical reasoning that may prove interesting:

  • When Einstein Walked With Gödel: Excursions to the Edge of Thought by Jim Holt
  • The Outer Limits of Reason: What Science, Mathematics, and Logic Cannot Tell Us by Noson S. Yanofsky
  • Ruelle's Essays
