Math 140: Elementary Analysis

This course provides a rigorous treatment of real analysis, the mathematical foundation of calculus.

Course Overview

Real analysis is the rigorous mathematical study of real numbers, sequences, functions, and continuity. In this course, students will explore:

  • Real numbers and their properties
  • Sequences and convergence
  • Series and infinite sums
  • Continuous functions
  • Derivatives and differentiability
  • Integrals and antiderivatives
  • Fundamental theorem of calculus

Key Concepts

Elementary analysis builds rigorous foundations for calculus:

  • Limits: The formalization of the intuitive notion of "approaching"
  • Continuity: Rigorous definition of unbroken curves and functions
  • Derivatives: Precise definition and properties of rates of change
  • Integrals: Rigorous theory of area and accumulation

Why Analysis Matters

While calculus provides tools for computation, analysis explains why these tools work:

  • Ensures mathematical rigor and prevents errors
  • Enables advanced topics in mathematics and physics
  • Provides foundations for functional analysis and differential equations
  • Essential for theoretical computer science and numerical analysis

Resources

Comprehensive course notes and problem sets with detailed solutions are available upon request. For supplementary reading, consult "Understanding Analysis" by Stephen Abbott or "Introduction to Real Analysis" by Robert Bartle and Donald Sherbert.
