MATH 120

Introduction to Abstract Algebra

Course Overview

This course provides an introduction to the fundamental structures of abstract algebra, exploring the beautiful abstractions that underlie algebraic systems.

Topics Covered

  • Group theory and group structures
  • Ring theory and polynomial rings
  • Field theory and field extensions
  • Homomorphisms and isomorphisms
  • Subgroups, subrings, and ideals
  • Quotient structures and isomorphism theorems

Key Concepts

Abstract algebra is the study of algebraic structures with operations. The course focuses on understanding fundamental properties:

  • Groups: Sets with an associative binary operation
  • Rings: Groups under addition with an additional multiplication operation
  • Fields: Rings where every nonzero element has a multiplicative inverse

Learning Objectives

After completing this course, students will be able to:

  • Understand and apply fundamental concepts in group, ring, and field theory
  • Work with homomorphisms and prove isomorphism theorems
  • Explore polynomial rings and field extensions
  • Solve abstract algebraic problems with rigor and precision

Resources

Comprehensive course notes and problem sets are available upon request. Recommended textbooks include:

  • Abstract Algebra by David S. Dummit and Richard M. Foote
  • Algebra by Michael Artin
  • Contemporary Abstract Algebra by Joseph Gallian
